Psychoeducational groups are structured programs that run for a set number of weeks and follow a planned curriculum. Each group is designed to help participants build skills and knowledge around a specific topic. Groups are organized by age (and sometimes by gender, depending on the focus of the group). When placing participants, we consider both how long someone has been waiting and how well the group members are likely to work together. Because these groups depend on community interest, they are not offered year-round. Some may run several times a year, while others may be offered only once annually.

**We are currently gathering interest for three groups:**

- EveryBody's Business
- Executive Functioning Rainbow
- Healthy Relationships Plus Program (HRPP)

#### EveryBody’s Business

For the most part, skills development for youth with differing abilities is based on a model that focuses on building skills to promote independence. One area where this is less likely to be the case is when discussing sexuality. For these youth, sexuality is more likely to be discussed retroactively (when an unwanted behaviour has occurred). Knowledge is power. Helping youth to understand both practical and social aspects of human sexuality can help equip them to keep themselves safer, and to better understand the social interactions they see around them and the feelings and changes they may be experiencing inside them.

This is a group for youth with differing abilities (generally diagnoses or symptoms consistent with diagnoses of Intellectual Disability or Autism Spectrum Disorder) aimed at teaching healthy sexuality. Topic complexity and detail vary based on the developmental level of the group, but generally include puberty, anatomy, public vs. private behaviour, sex and the law/consent, intimate relationships, personal sexual identity (gender/orientation) and socially appropriate behaviour in responding to others, and sexual safety. Groups will be created with developmental levels of the youth in mind and will proceed when enough participants within that developmental level express interest.

In order to participate in the group, parents/caregivers must participate in a 3-hour parent seminar which discusses the format of the group, risks and benefits, and how parents can impact and support learning. This is a group that operates by completing a syllabus and is closed to new participants once it begins. EveryBody’s Business includes 10 sessions 90 minutes in length in addition to the parent seminar. Youth must be able to read and write in order to participate in the group due to the nature of some of the activities.

#### Executive Functioning Rainbow

Executive functioning skills represent the strategies that we use to regulate our thoughts, emotions and behaviours. Without them, we can struggle to focus, to organize ourselves both inside our heads and in our daily lives, to control our impulses and to get started on things we have to do. The good news is that these skills can improve with practice, once we understand what they are and how they work.

This group is currently a pilot project. It supports the learning of specific executive functioning skills during an 8-week closed group (completing a pre-designed syllabus) for children aged 7 to 10. Children will engage in activities during each of 8 sessions that focus on a particular executive functioning skill and link this skill to their daily lives. Sessions are 75 minutes in length and include a 15-minute caregiver debrief in order to discuss the skill and at-home practice.

#### The Healthy Relationships Plus Program (HRPP)

A small-group program for youth that uses interactive activities to help teens learn important life skills—like spotting early signs of unhealthy relationships, understanding how influences shape our choices, communicating clearly, setting personal boundaries, and handling emotions. It additionally focuses on mental health, suicide prevention, and drug and alcohol awareness—all aimed at helping teens build positive, healthy relationships. It's a proactive, strengths-based approach designed to give youth the tools to make safer, healthier choices.
