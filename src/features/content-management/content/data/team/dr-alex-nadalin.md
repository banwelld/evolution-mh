As a child, I was drawn to the idea of becoming a detective – someone who gathers clues, asks thoughtful questions, and pieces together information to better understand what’s really going on. That same curiosity continues to shape how I approach my work today.

I am a registered clinical and school psychologist serving children and adolescents in Ontario. Much of my work focuses on helping families understand why a child may be struggling at school, experiencing overwhelming emotions, or having difficulty coping in challenging moments, as well as whether they may be developing differently than expected.

This detective spirit is at the heart of my comprehensive assessment practice. I bring together information about a child’s development, learning, behaviour, and emotional functioning to build a clear picture of their unique profile. I work collaboratively with children, parents, and caregivers to identify strengths and areas of need, and to translate assessment findings into meaningful, actionable guidance. I place particular emphasis on presenting findings in clear, everyday language so that parents and young people can feel informed and confident moving forward.

In addition to assessment, I provide treatment for children and adolescents navigating anxiety, mood difficulties, social struggles, and life stressors. My approach blends empathy and insight with practical tools, and I involve parents throughout the process to support change outside sessions. I also have experience facilitating and evaluating psychoeducational groups, offering a structured and supportive space to build skills alongside peers.

I completed my Ph.D. in School and Applied Child Psychology at the University of Western Ontario. I have trained and worked in school, community mental health, and private practice settings. I also currently work with a local school board where I provide assessment and consultation services to support students and their families in educational planning. Having been born and raised in the Windsor–Essex community, I am especially grateful for the opportunity to provide support in the region I call home.

If you’re looking for clarity, direction, or a better understanding of your child, I’d be glad to connect.
