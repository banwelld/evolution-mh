# Therapy

###### Evidence-Based Interventions

Because everyone's story is unique, we tailor therapy to your goals, strengths, and challenges. We see therapy as a collaborative process and we take a client-centred approach, meaning your therapist will work with you to find an approach that feels like the right fit, rather than a one-size-fits-all model.

Your first session is a chance for you and your therapist to get to know each other. We'll talk about what brings you to therapy, discuss confidentiality and its limits, benefits and risks related to therapy, and begin to set goals together. This may include the use of questionnaires and other assessment tools as needed. Therapy methods and techniques will vary depending on the goals you would like to address. You and your therapist will also decide on a schedule that fits your needs and availability. Therapy is offered virtually or in-person at our office.

### Individual Therapy

Individual therapy involves meeting one-on-one with a therapist to explore your concerns, set goals together, and work toward living a more meaningful and fulfilling life. Your therapist will support you in exploring your thoughts, feelings, and experiences, and you'll work together to decide how to best use therapy for growth and positive change.

Sometimes, people who are important in your life—such as family members or partners—may be invited to join a session. This would only happen with your agreement and in collaboration with your therapist. If the client is a child or adolescent, we begin by discussing with parents or caregivers how privacy will be handled in sessions, so that the young person feels both supported and respected.

### Couples/Intimate Relationship Therapy

Intimate relationship therapy supports couples or people in a close relationship who want to strengthen and improve their connection. This may include building healthier communication, setting respectful boundaries, and creating routines that support a stronger relationship.

We understand how sexual health can impact intimacy. While we are not registered sex therapists, however, if your main concern is sexual dysfunction, we can connect you with a qualified specialist in that area.

### Family Therapy

Family therapy is designed to help family members better understand each other, improve communication, and work through patterns that may be causing stress or conflict. This service is not limited to parents and young children—adult children, siblings, or whole families can also benefit from therapy together. In sessions, the family works as a unit, with everyone's voice respected.
